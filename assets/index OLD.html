The changing nature of work

You’re on placement. Each megatrend is a different challenge. Megatrends are large, global forces that impact how we live, work, communicate, and participate in society. In CEM you investigate four megatrends and how they affect the nature of work: work environments, types of employment, and emerging and declining jobs.

Why this matters for you

One day your job might not exist. The next, a job that doesn’t exist yet might be yours. The question isn’t whether work will change—it’s whether you’ll see it coming.

When industrialisation hit, people left the land: farming shrank, factories boomed. Automated checkouts pushed operator roles down—and sent delivery and click-and-collect packing roles up. Banks closed branches and cut tellers; home lenders and call centres grew. The work didn’t disappear. It moved. The types of jobs and the skills they need—that’s what keeps shifting.

This is about staying in the front seat. Seeing the curve before it hits. Anticipating, preparing, and grabbing the opportunities—instead of being blindsided by redundancies and "why didn’t anyone tell me?" It’s about learning to read what’s happening, spot threats and opportunities, and ask: What does this mean for me? Reskill? Upskill? Change direction?

In this game you’ll step into the pressure. Real scenarios, real stakes. Your choices will build—or cost you—the skills employers look for. You’re not just learning about megatrends. You’re practising the thinking that keeps you in the front seat.

Impactful technology — summary

Work environments: Growth in remote work, telecommuting and working from home; digitisation and automation change how and where people work.

Types of employment: Increased workplace flexibility; growth in gig work, freelance and contract roles; hybrid and remote arrangements.

Emerging jobs: Systems developer, data analyst, AI trainer, digital support roles. Declining jobs: Many administrative, clerical and routine data-entry roles as tasks are automated.

In small organisations (e.g. local firm), staff may need to upskill in cloud-based software (Xero, MYOB) and digital literacy. In large organisations (e.g. mining), new roles include drone operators, AI technicians and remote operations; some traditional roles are replaced by automation.
